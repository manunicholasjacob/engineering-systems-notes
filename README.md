# Engineering Systems Notes

This repository contains short essays on engineering systems, design, and reliability.

The focus is on systems-level reasoning: how complex systems behave under constraints, how failure modes emerge over time, and how design decisions shape reliability long before issues surface.

These notes are intentionally abstract and pattern-based. They are not tied to any specific company, product, or incident, and are meant to remain broadly applicable across hardware, software, organizations, and processes.

Additional essays may be added over time as these themes are explored further.

## Scope and Intent

These notes are written from the perspective of an engineer developing a deeper understanding of systems behavior.

They aim to:
- Emphasize structure over anecdotes  
- Focus on patterns rather than prescriptions  
- Treat constraints, feedback, and interactions as first-class design concerns  

They are not intended as tutorials, playbooks, or postmortems.
